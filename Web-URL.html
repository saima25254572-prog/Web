About URL Encoding/Decoding

URL encoding converts characters into a format that can be transmitted over the Internet. URLs can only be sent over the Internet using the ASCII character set.

Since URLs often contain characters outside the ASCII set, they must be converted to a valid ASCII format. URL encoding replaces unsafe ASCII characters with a "%" followed by two hexadecimal digits.

Common characters that need encoding include spaces (replaced with %20), ampersands (& replaced with %26), and question marks (? replaced with %3F).
