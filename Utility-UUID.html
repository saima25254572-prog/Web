About UUIDs

  • UUID stands for Universally Unique Identifier
  • Version 4 UUIDs are randomly generated and most commonly used
  • Version 1 UUIDs are based on timestamp and MAC address
  • UUIDs are 128-bit numbers, usually represented as 32 hexadecimal digits
  • They are used to uniquely identify information in computer systems
